Plutarch says that Vercingetorix put on his finest armor and upon his
war horse came forth from the town. After riding around Caesar several
times as he sat in his tribunal, he dismounted, took off his armor, and seated
himself at Caesar's feet. He sat there in silence till Caesar ordered him to
be removed and kept for his triumph in Rome. There he was led in the
triumpha] procession and afterwards put to death in prison.
